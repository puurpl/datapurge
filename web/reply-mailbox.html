The Reply Mailbox

A private inbox alias for contacting data collectors. Your personal inbox stays out of data-broker hands: the alias is the contact address brokers keep on file, the address you type into their request forms, and the place their replies, verification loops and marketing-list abuse land - with pause and block controls and a private evidence trail.

Why give a broker your real email at all?

To make a broker delete you, you have to contact them, and the strongest requests ask them to reply in writing. That means handing a data broker a working address - the exact thing you are trying to keep out of their hands. Some brokers only accept requests through a web form, which also asks for a contact email. Once they have your real inbox, it becomes another record to sell, and their replies, endless verification loops, and any list abuse land in the inbox you use every day.

The Reply Mailbox breaks that trade-off. You get a private alias, something like u-abc23xyz@your-relay-domain, and you give brokers that instead. It becomes the contact address they keep on file and type into their forms. Their mail is received, sorted, and forwarded to your real inbox, and each reply is tracked in your DataPurge progress view so you can see who acknowledged, who asked for verification, and who confirmed removal.

📧

The relay only ever emails you

We never email brokers on your behalf. Every opt-out request is still sent by you, from your own account. The relay's only job is to receive broker mail and forward it to you, so nothing here turns DataPurge into an agent acting in your name.

Controls you hold

Pause and block

Pause the alias whenever you like, or block a specific broker domain that will not stop. You stay in control of what reaches you.

An evidence trail

Every reply is kept as your proof: what a broker said, when, and how it was classified. It squares "keep the evidence" with "read it only yourself".

Readable only by you

Message bodies are sealed to a key generated in your browser and never sent to us. We hold ciphertext only, and you get a one-time key backup.

What is stored, plainly

A privacy tool should explain its own data handling in plain language. Here it is.

  • For each broker reply we store the subject line, a classification (such as acknowledged, verification required, or completed), timestamps, and the sealed message body. Subjects are kept in plain text so your reply list is readable; bodies are sealed to your browser-held key and readable only by you.
  • We keep these records as your evidence trail until you delete them. Deleting your alias erases the alias and every stored reply and its evidence.
  • Relay traffic is carried by Cloudflare Email Routing and Email Sending. Cloudflare retains roughly 31 days of from/to/subject metadata for that mail, is contractually barred from using message content for anything beyond delivering the service, and full-body preview storage is disabled on our sending domain. With preview disabled, Cloudflare keeps no message bodies beyond transient processing.
  • We never claim that nobody ever sees anything. The accurate, narrower claim is the one above: Cloudflare cannot use your mail beyond delivering it, and what we store is readable only by you.

One honest caveat

When you send opt-out emails yourself - for example the mass BCC batches - those still go out from your own email client and show your own sending address as the From. The alias governs where brokers write back and what they keep on file, and anything you submit through a broker's web form using the alias exposes nothing personal. It protects the address brokers hold, not the address you happen to send from.

The full data-handling terms live on the privacy policy, versioned in the open alongside the code.
