Privacy Policy

A privacy tool should be able to explain its own data handling in one page. Here it is. Last updated: July 5, 2026.

The short version

Everything you type into DataPurge stays in your browser. The site has no accounts, no analytics scripts, no tracking pixels, and no third-party embeds. The only personal data we ever hold is what you explicitly hand to the optional services you choose to sign up for - the email reminder service or the Reply Mailbox - and you can delete that at any time.

Using the app

Your profile (name, email, address, date of birth, and any other fields you fill in) is stored in your browser's localStorage on your device. It is never transmitted to us. Opt-out emails are assembled locally in your browser and sent from your own email client - DataPurge never sees them. Clearing your browser data or using the in-app profile controls removes it.

One practical note: because your data lives in localStorage unencrypted, treat shared or public computers with care - use a private window or delete your profile when you're done.

The optional email reminder service ("drip")

If you sign up for daily opt-out reminder emails, we store the minimum needed to build them:

  • your signup email address;
  • one copy of your filled opt-out text (which contains the personal details you chose to include, such as your name and location);
  • the list of brokers in your queue and a log of which reminder emails we've sent you;
  • whether you ticked the optional updates box.

Signups use double opt-in: until you click the confirmation link, the only thing we ever send is that single confirmation email, and unconfirmed signups are deleted automatically after 7 days.

If you ticked the optional updates box, we may occasionally email you about online privacy news, DataPurge features, and related privacy services we offer. That is the only marketing use of your address, it only happens with that box ticked, and unsubscribing ends it.

These reminder emails go to you and only you. We never email brokers on your behalf - every opt-out request is sent by you, from your own email account.

When you unsubscribe (one click in any email), we immediately delete your opt-out text, broker queue, and send history. We keep only your email address, marked inactive, so we never email you again. If you want even that removed, open a GitHub issue or contact us and we'll erase it.

The optional Reply Mailbox service

The Reply Mailbox is a separate, paid, opt-in service. It gives you a private inbox alias to hand data brokers instead of your own address. The alias becomes the contact address brokers keep on file and type into their request forms, and it is where their replies, verification requests, and any marketing-list abuse land - with pause and block controls and a private evidence trail. It exists so that maintaining your privacy while contacting data collectors does not mean exposing your personal inbox to them.

Subscribing is two things at once, and both are true: it is a working private mail tool, and it is a contribution to the project's fight against blanket surveillance. It keeps DataPurge free for everyone, and it covers running costs and time rather than turning a profit.

For each broker reply we store the subject line, a classification (for example acknowledged, verification required, or completed), timestamps, and the sealed message body. The body is encrypted to a key generated in your browser and never sent to us, so stored bodies are readable only by you - we and our infrastructure hold ciphertext only. We keep these records as your evidence trail until you delete them; deleting your alias erases the alias and every stored reply and its evidence.

Relay traffic is carried by Cloudflare Email Routing and Email Sending. Cloudflare retains roughly 31 days of from/to/subject metadata for that mail, is contractually barred from using message content for anything beyond delivering the service, and full-body preview storage is disabled on our sending domain. This is the accurate, narrower claim: with preview disabled, Cloudflare keeps no message bodies beyond transient processing, and what we store is readable only by you.

As with the reminder service, the Reply Mailbox only ever emails you. We never email brokers on your behalf. One honest caveat: when you send opt-out emails yourself (for example the mass BCC batches), those still go out from your own email client and show your own sending address as the From. The alias governs where brokers write back and what they keep on file, and anything you submit through a broker's web form using the alias exposes nothing personal.

Infrastructure

The site is served by Cloudflare Pages, and the reminder service runs on Cloudflare Workers with data stored in Cloudflare D1. Reminder emails are delivered through Resend. Like any host, these providers process standard connection logs (such as IP addresses) to serve and protect the site; we add no logging of our own and have no analytics.

Affiliate links on the Paid Services page

Our Paid Services page reviews third-party data-removal services, and some of the outbound links there are affiliate links. If you click one and sign up, that service may pay us a commission at no extra cost to you. This is how the page funds itself, so we can keep the DataPurge tool free for everyone.

We mark affiliate links with an affiliate tag, and a commission never affects a rating or ranking - we report what we see, link or not. We don't add any tracking of our own to these links; clicking one takes you to the third party's own site, where their privacy policy (and any cookies they set) applies. We never share your personal information with these services - you deal with them directly.

Not legal advice

DataPurge generates request letters that cite privacy laws, and helps you track responses. This is information and tooling, not legal advice, and using DataPurge does not create an attorney–client relationship. Laws vary by jurisdiction and change over time; for advice about your specific situation, consult a lawyer.
