Kanary review

Green - recommended Trust-first pick · reviewed July 2026

Bottom line

If your first question is "who am I actually trusting with my data?", Kanary has the cleanest answer on this page. It's an independent startup backed by privacy-aligned investors including Mozilla and Y Combinator, its privacy policy is a short, blunt "we never sell, share, or disclose your personal information," and it offers a genuinely free tier. It also goes further than most into high-risk territory - doxxing, stalking, deepfakes.

The trade-off is effectiveness: Consumer Reports rated Kanary a mid-level performer, and its fully-managed removal tier is pricey at $50/month. Kanary has no affiliate program, so we earn nothing from this recommendation.

Who owns it - and can you trust them?

Kanary is run by Kanaries Inc., an independent startup founded in 2020 in Chicago by Rachel Vrabec. Its backers are notable for a data-removal company: Y Combinator, Mozilla, 2048, and First Star - privacy-aligned money rather than ad-tech or data-broker capital. We found no evidence its owner operates any data broker, people-search, or ad-data business.

No, it wasn't bought by Yahoo

You may run into a claim online that Kanary was "acquired by Yahoo in 2023." We checked Yahoo's own acquisition history and the M&A databases and found no such deal - the story appears to be a mix-up with an unrelated, older company that shared the name. Kanary remains independent as of 2026.

Its privacy policy is refreshingly short and human-readable, and it commits to never selling, sharing, or disclosing your personal information (aggregated, non-personal statistics for researchers/regulators excepted). For a category built on handling sensitive data, that combination - aligned owners plus a minimal, blunt policy - is the strongest trust story here.

Does it actually work?

Kanary was one of only seven services Consumer Reports put through its 2024 controlled test, and it landed as a mid-level performer - roughly on par with DeleteMe and IDX. That's neither the best nor the worst: better than the bottom of the field, but well short of Optery and EasyOptOuts. If raw removal percentage is your only metric, those two are stronger.

Where Kanary stands out is scope. It explicitly handles higher-risk exposure most competitors ignore - doxxing, stalking, deepfakes, executive protection, and search-engine delisting - which can matter far more than a raw broker count if you're being targeted. Note the tiering, though: on the free and Professional plans the removals are "self-driven" (Kanary finds and guides, you act); only the $50/month Advanced tier hands the work to Kanary's team.

Your privacy & data

To run scans, Kanary asks for the basics needed to find you - name, contact details, and location such as city/state. Application and server logs are deleted after about a week; account data is retained while your account is active to improve scan accuracy, and deleted when you close the account. Hosting is on AWS. The headline commitment - never selling, sharing, or disclosing personal data - is stated plainly rather than buried in legalese.

Security

Kanary states SOC 2 compliance and lists GDPR and NIST CSF 2.0 alignment, AES-256 encryption, PBKDF2 password hashing, and optional two-factor auth, and we found no breach history. Two honest caveats: the SOC 2 claim is self-reported (the underlying audit report isn't public, which is normal), and Kanary itself is not ISO 27001 certified - that certification belongs to its AWS hosting layer, not to Kanary.
