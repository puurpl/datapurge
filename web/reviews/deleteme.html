DeleteMe review

Orange - trade-offs Reviewed July 2026

Independent (Abine, Inc.) · Boston, US · since 2010

Bottom line

DeleteMe is the oldest and most trusted brand in this space, and on the questions of ownership and security it's genuinely strong: independently owned, no data-broker conflict, SOC 2 since 2021 and ISO 27001 certified, with human-assisted removals. The problem is the one that matters most - results. In Consumer Reports' controlled 2024 study it removed only about 27% of test exposures in four months, well behind Optery and EasyOptOuts and below even doing it yourself for free. For a service that starts around $129/year with no free trial, that's a real caveat.

Who owns it - and can you trust them?

DeleteMe is run by Abine, Inc. ("The Online Privacy Company"), an independent, privately held company founded in 2010 by Rob Shavell (still CEO), Eugene Kuznetsov, and Andrew Sudbury. It raised a single $5.2M Series A back in 2011 and has no record of a private-equity buyout. Its other products - Blur (since spun out as IronVest) and Block Party (acquired in 2026) - are consumer-privacy tools, not data-selling businesses.

No "both ends" conflict

We found nothing linking Abine, its founders, or its investors to a data broker or people-search operation - the conflict that has damaged trust in some rivals. On the ownership question specifically, DeleteMe is on solid ground.

Does it actually work?

Here's where DeleteMe's reputation and its measured performance part ways. In Consumer Reports' August 2024 study - the most rigorous independent test available - DeleteMe removed roughly 27% of test profiles within four months. That's a mid-level result: better than the very worst services, but far behind Optery (68%) and EasyOptOuts (65%), and below the ~70% that volunteers achieved doing opt-outs manually for free.

DeleteMe's coverage numbers (variously cited as 750+, 950+, or 3,610+ sites) are inconsistent and likely reflect maximum theoretical reach rather than what any single plan actually monitors. The human-assisted process is a genuine plus for hard-to-remove listings; just don't expect the brand name to translate into best-in-class removal rates.

Your privacy & data

To do its job, DeleteMe needs a fairly complete identity picture - name, current and past addresses, aliases, emails and phones - but it never requires your SSN, and family, employer, and government-ID fields are optional. It states clearly that it does not sell your data. The two things to note: its policy contains the standard clause allowing your data to transfer in a merger, acquisition, or bankruptcy (Consumer Reports called this out specifically), and it retains account data for about three years after you cancel.

Security

On paper this is the strongest security posture among the independents: SOC 2 Type 2 maintained annually since 2021, ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certification, TX-RAMP, and a public TrustShare transparency portal, plus the usual AES-256/TLS/MFA/RBAC controls. One honest correction to the "spotless record" narrative some reviews repeat: Abine's sibling product Blur suffered a 2019 cloud-storage misconfiguration that exposed data on roughly 2.4 million users. It wasn't the DeleteMe service itself, but it's the same company, and worth knowing.
