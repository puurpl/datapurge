Bottom line

Optery is the rare service that does well on both things that matter: trust and results. It's independently owned with no data-broker ties, SOC 2 Type II audited, and it was the single most effective consumer service in Consumer Reports' 2024 test. It even offers a free scan and a free tier. It's our top pick - and, in the interest of full disclosure, the one service on this page that currently pays us a commission.

We want to be square with you about that. Optery earns the top spot on the evidence, not the commission - and we've placed EasyOptOuts, which pays us nothing and matches Optery's effectiveness at a fraction of the price, right beside it as the better buy for many people. If Optery's evidence ever slips, so will its rating.

Who owns it - and can you trust them?

This is the question that matters most for a company you're about to hand your home address and relatives to, and Optery answers it well. It's an independent, venture-backed startup (Y Combinator's W22 batch, plus a roster of VCs), co-founded in 2020 by CEO Lawrence Gentilello, CTO Chen Atlas, and Dekel Barzilay. There's no parent conglomerate, and as of April 2026 it was still independent and privately held.

No "both ends" conflict found

Optery states plainly that it "does not sell data, and has never in the past sold data to any organization, including data brokers," and has "never… had any financial relationship or affiliate partnership" with them. No independent reporting ties Optery to a data broker or people-search site - in fact Optery was calling out a competitor's conflicts back in 2021, years before KrebsOnSecurity's 2024 investigation confirmed them.

One bit of background worth knowing: before Optery, Gentilello led Oracle's BlueKai ad-data practice - an audience-data business adjacent, in spirit, to the industry Optery now helps you escape. That's disclosed prior employment, not a current stake or sibling company, and it doesn't amount to an active conflict. We mention it only so you have the full picture.

Does it actually work?

Optery has the best independent evidence in the category. In Consumer Reports' 2024 controlled study - which tested 13 services with real volunteers and found the industry "largely ineffective" overall - Optery removed 68% of exposures within four months, the highest score of any service tested. It has also been a repeat PCMag Editors' Choice pick since 2022.

Keep expectations honest, though: 68% is best-in-class, but it still means roughly a third of your known exposures were not gone after four months. No paid service is a one-and-done fix, which is exactly why the ongoing monitoring matters. Optery's own coverage and site-count numbers (up to 940+ sites on the top tier) are self-reported.

Your privacy & data

You can run a free "Quick Scan" with no account at all (results auto-delete after 14 days). Paid removal needs the identifiers required to match your records at brokers - name, addresses, phone, aliases, relatives - and offers government-ID upload as an optional verification step. There's no mandatory SSN. Data is processed in the US.

The one asterisk: Optery's own privacy policy discloses that it runs retargeted advertising with vendors like Google, and acknowledges this "may be considered selling or sharing" under California law. It's opt-out-able via Global Privacy Control signals, and it's separate from the core removal data - but if you're the kind of person reading this page, you'll want to know.

Security

Optery holds an AICPA SOC 2 Type II certification (audited by Prescient Assurance, first announced in 2023 and maintained since), and publishes real detail: AES-256 at rest, TLS in transit, mandatory MFA for production, zero-trust access, and annual third-party penetration testing. We found no evidence of an Optery breach as of mid-2026. One honest caveat: Optery itself is not ISO 27001 certified - only its AWS hosting layer is - so treat any "ISO 27001 certified" claim you see in third-party marketing with care.
