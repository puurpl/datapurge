# Acknowledgments

The DataPurge broker registry is built on the collective work of privacy researchers,
journalists, advocates, and open-source contributors who have spent years documenting
data brokers and their opt-out processes. This file credits the projects and resources
that made our registry possible.

## Primary Source Lists

### Big Ass Data Broker Opt-Out List (BADBOOL)
- **Author:** Yael Grauer
- **URL:** https://github.com/yaelwrites/Big-Ass-Data-Broker-Opt-Out-List
- **License:** CC BY-NC-SA (Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike)
- **Description:** The most comprehensive and actively maintained open-source list of
  data broker opt-out instructions. Covers 100+ brokers organized by priority, with
  detailed step-by-step removal instructions, difficulty ratings, and notes on
  verification requirements. This was our single most valuable source.

### glamrock/data-brokers
- **Author:** Griffin Boyce
- **URL:** https://github.com/glamrock/data-brokers
- **License:** CC0 (Public Domain)
- **Description:** A compiled list of data aggregators, data brokers, direct marketing
  firms, credit reporting agencies, and others who trade in personal data, along with
  opt-out instructions. Organized by category (credit agencies, people finders,
  marketing, telemarketing). One of the earliest open-source broker lists.

## Government Registries

### California Data Broker Registry (CPPA)
- **URL:** https://cppa.ca.gov/data_broker_registry/
- **Maintained by:** California Privacy Protection Agency
- **Description:** Official registry of data brokers required to register under
  California law. Available as downloadable CSV. As of 2026, also home to the DELETE
  Request and Opt-Out Platform (DROP), a free tool allowing California residents to
  submit deletion requests to all registered brokers simultaneously.
- **Previous registry (2021-2023):** https://oag.ca.gov/data-brokers

### Vermont Data Broker Registry
- **URL:** https://sos.vermont.gov/corporations/other-services/data-brokers/
- **Maintained by:** Vermont Secretary of State
- **Description:** Vermont was the first US state to require data broker registration
  (effective 2019). Annual registration with a searchable public database. Brokers pay
  a $100 annual fee and must disclose data collection and opt-out practices.

### Texas Data Broker Registry
- **Maintained by:** Texas Secretary of State
- **Description:** Texas requires data broker registration under its data privacy law.
  Cross-referenced with other state registries by Privacy Rights Clearinghouse.

### Oregon Data Broker Registry
- **Maintained by:** Oregon Department of Consumer and Business Services
- **Description:** Oregon's data broker registration requirement, part of the state's
  expanding privacy law framework.

## Research Organizations and Databases

### Privacy Rights Clearinghouse
- **URL:** https://privacyrights.org/data-brokers
- **Description:** In 2025, Privacy Rights Clearinghouse built a unified database of
  750+ data brokers by collecting and cross-referencing registration data from all five
  US state registries. Their research identified hundreds of companies that registered
  in one state but failed to register in others. An essential reference for
  understanding the full data broker landscape.

### DataBrokersWatch.org
- **URL:** https://databrokerswatch.org/
- **Description:** Maintained in collaboration with YourDigitalRights.org, this project
  tracks major data brokers and provides one-click deletion request tools. Their top-ten
  list and opt-out redirects through yourdigitalrights.org were used to verify broker
  details.

## Guides and Workbooks

### IntelTechniques Personal Data Removal Workbook
- **Author:** Michael Bazzell
- **URL:** https://inteltechniques.com/workbook.html
- **Description:** A free PDF workbook with removal instructions for dozens of people
  search engines and data brokers. Part of the Extreme Privacy methodology. Each entry
  includes the service name, website, direct removal link, contact options, and process
  notes. Updated regularly (last update October 2024).

### DeleteMe DIY Free Opt-Out Guide

- **Maintained by:** Abine / JoinDeleteMe
- **Description:** Individual opt-out guides for major data brokers with step-by-step

  registry.

- **URL:** https://blog.incogni.com/opt-out-guides/
- **Maintained by:** Surfshark / Incogni

### Optery Opt-Out Instructions

## How We Use These Sources

Each broker entry in the DataPurge registry includes a `meta.notes` field that credits
the specific sources used to compile that entry. Our process:

1. **Cross-reference** broker names across multiple source lists
2. **Verify** opt-out URLs and methods against current broker websites
3. **Document** opt-out steps, difficulty, and processing times
4. **Track** legal compliance (CCPA, state registrations) using government registries
5. **Monitor** for changes, as broker opt-out processes frequently change

## Contributing

The data broker landscape changes constantly. New brokers appear, opt-out URLs break,
and companies merge or rebrand. If you find outdated information or know of a broker
we have not covered:

1. Open an issue or pull request on our GitHub repository
2. Include the broker name, domain, opt-out method, and your source
3. If possible, verify the opt-out process works before submitting

We especially welcome contributions from:
- Privacy researchers and journalists
- Users who have completed opt-out processes and can confirm current steps
- Legal professionals familiar with state data privacy laws
- International contributors who can document brokers operating outside the US
